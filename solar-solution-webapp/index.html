Engineering diagram of 60A single-phase houses on shared feeder with ADMD calculations

Understanding ADMD (After Diversity Maximum Demand)

Definition: ADMD is the maximum power demand that a group of consumers will simultaneously place on the electrical network after considering diversity. It accounts for the statistical reality that not all consumers use their maximum demand at the same time.

Why ADMD Matters: If we sized transformers based on the sum of all individual maximum demands, the infrastructure would be vastly oversized and economically unviable. ADMD allows for realistic, cost-effective network planning.

ADMD Calculation Example:

  • • Individual House Max Demand: 13.8 kVA (60A @ 230V)
  • • Total Installed Demand (57 houses): 18 × 13.8 = 248.4 kVA
  • • ADMD Factor (Residential): 0.35 - 0.45 (typically 0.4)
  • • Actual Maximum Demand: 248.4 × 0.4 = 314.6 kVA
  • • Transformer Size Required: 315 kVA ✓

Network Planning Application: Using ADMD factor of 0.4, a 315 kVA mini-sub transformer can reliably serve approximately 57 houses with 60A single-phase supply, even though their combined maximum demand would theoretically be 248.4 kVA.
